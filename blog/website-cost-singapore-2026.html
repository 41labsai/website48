How Much Does a Website Cost in Singapore in 2026?

By Website48 5 min read Feb 3, 2026

If you've ever Googled "website cost Singapore," you've probably seen quotes ranging from $0 to $50,000. It's enough to make any business owner's head spin. Whether you're running a hawker stall looking to take online orders, an HDB home-based business, or a growing SME, understanding what you're actually paying for is crucial.

The truth is, web design pricing in Singapore varies wildly because you're not just paying for a website — you're paying for different combinations of time, expertise, and ongoing commitments. Let's break down exactly what each option costs, so you can make an informed decision for your business.

DIY Website Builders: $0–$50/month

Platforms like Wix, Squarespace, and WordPress.com have made it possible for anyone to build a website. On the surface, this seems like the cheapest option for getting a cheap website in Singapore. But let's look at the real costs:

  • Free plans: Come with platform branding, limited features, and subdomains (yoursite.wixsite.com) — not exactly professional
  • Paid plans: $15–$50/month for decent features, which adds up to $180–$600 per year
  • Premium themes: $50–$200 one-time (the free ones look... free)
  • Apps and plugins: $5–$30/month each for booking systems, email marketing, etc.
  • Your time: 20–40+ hours learning the platform and building your site

The hidden cost nobody talks about? Your time. If you're spending weekends wrestling with drag-and-drop builders instead of running your business, that's a real cost. For a busy SME owner in Singapore, those hours could be spent serving customers or growing your business.

DIY verdict

Good for hobbyists and those who genuinely enjoy web design. Not ideal if you value your time or want a polished, professional result.

Freelancers: $500–$2,000

Hiring a freelance web designer in Singapore is a popular middle-ground option. Here's what you can typically expect:

Price Range What You Get
$500–$800 Basic template-based site, 3–5 pages, minimal customization
$800–$1,500 Custom design, 5–8 pages, some unique features
$1,500–$2,000 Fully custom design, more pages, basic e-commerce

Pros of hiring a freelancer

  • More affordable than agencies
  • Direct communication with the person building your site
  • Can find specialists in your industry

Cons of hiring a freelancer

  • Quality varies dramatically — some are excellent, others not so much
  • May disappear or become unresponsive (it happens more than you'd think)
  • Limited availability for ongoing support
  • No backup if they get sick or busy with other projects

Finding a reliable freelancer in Singapore can be hit or miss. Check portfolios carefully, read reviews, and always get a written contract.

Web Design Agencies: $3,000–$15,000+

Agencies are the premium option in the web design pricing Singapore landscape. They offer comprehensive services, but at a significant cost:

  • $3,000–$5,000: Smaller agencies, template-based with customization
  • $5,000–$10,000: Mid-tier agencies, custom design, more features
  • $10,000–$15,000+: Established agencies, fully bespoke design, advanced functionality

When agencies make sense

If you're a larger company with complex requirements — custom integrations, multiple languages, advanced e-commerce with thousands of products, or specific compliance needs — an agency might be worth the investment. They offer project management, dedicated teams, and ongoing support infrastructure.

When they don't

For most Singapore SMEs, hawker businesses, or home-based businesses, spending $5,000–$10,000 on a website is overkill. You're essentially paying for office overheads, project managers, and lengthy processes that a small business doesn't need.

What Affects Website Pricing?

Regardless of which option you choose, certain factors will influence the final cost:

Number of pages

A simple 5-page site is much cheaper than a 50-page site. Most small businesses in Singapore need 5–10 pages maximum.

E-commerce functionality

Selling products online adds complexity. Basic online stores add $200–$500 to most quotes; complex e-commerce with inventory management can add thousands.

Custom features

Booking systems, membership areas, custom calculators, or integrations with existing software all increase costs significantly.

Content creation

If you need professional photography, copywriting, or video, factor that into your budget — it's often not included in web design quotes.

Ongoing maintenance

Some options require monthly hosting and maintenance fees. Others (like Website48) include everything upfront.
