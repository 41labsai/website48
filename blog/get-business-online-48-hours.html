What You Need Before Starting

Before you begin, gather these essentials. Having them ready will dramatically speed up your launch.

  • Business name and any registered business details (UEN if applicable)
  • Logo (even a simple one works to start)
  • 5-10 photos of your business, products, or team
  • Contact information: phone, email, address, WhatsApp number
  • Brief description of what you do and who you serve
  • Operating hours (if relevant)

Do not have a logo yet? Do not let that stop you. You can start with your business name in a clean font and upgrade later.

1

Choose Your Domain Name

Your domain is your address on the internet. Choose wisely, but do not overthink it.

.com vs .sg: Which Should You Pick?

  • .com is universally recognised and works well if you might expand beyond Singapore or want a more international feel.
  • .com.sg signals local credibility and is great for businesses serving primarily Singapore customers. It also helps with local SEO.
  • .sg is shorter and modern, good for tech-savvy audiences.

Pro tip: Keep your domain short, easy to spell, and avoid hyphens or numbers. "brightsmileclinic.sg" beats "bright-smile-dental-clinic-sg.com" every time.

2

Prepare Your Content

Content is what makes your website actually useful. Here is the minimum you need:

Essential Pages for a Quick Launch

  1. Home page: Who you are, what you do, and why customers should choose you
  2. About page: Your story, experience, and what makes you different
  3. Services/Products page: What you offer with clear descriptions
  4. Contact page: Multiple ways to reach you (phone, email, WhatsApp, location)

Photos That Work

  • Real photos of your business perform better than stock images
  • Smartphone photos in good lighting are perfectly acceptable
  • Show your workspace, products, team, or yourself at work
  • Aim for at least 5 quality images

4

Review and Launch

Before going live, run through this quick review:

  • All contact information is correct
  • Phone numbers and links work when clicked
  • Business name and address are accurate
  • Photos look good on both desktop and mobile
  • The overall message represents your business well

Do not aim for perfection. Aim for "good enough to launch." You can always refine later. A live website that is 90% perfect beats a perfect website that never launches.

5

Tell the World

Your website is live. Now make sure people can find it.

Immediate Actions (Day 1)
